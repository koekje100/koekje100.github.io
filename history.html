History

The history of watches goes some time back in history. Usually most people agree that Peter Henlein is the father of portable clocks, though there is no direct evidence he was the first.
Watches evolved from accessoires for the rich instead of actual useful devices that could accurately tell the time. They were so inaccurate that they were basicly useless.

Some time later there were portable spring-driven clocks. These watches were just basic devices that still inaccurately showed the time. Some speculate that watches were called 'watches', because mostly watchmen used to keep track of their shifts.

In 1657 though, a revolutionary invention, the 'balance spring',
improved the watch's accuracy to only be a few minutes off
each day, instead of a few hours. This also made the minute
hand a possibility. This caused a two-century lasting innovation
period where watches were constantly improving.

In the coming century watches became pretty accurate devices that could be easily carried with you, without being bothered by for example temperature or movement. Still, for the most part watches were worn by the elite, mainly because manufacturing was still done by hand.
That changed in the mid-19th century. In the United States someone named Aaron Lufkin Dennison opened up shop in 1851 and in 1861 he was running a succesful enterprise.

From 1920 technology improved very fast. In WW1 there was a great nead for sturdy wristwatches, because synchronization between artillery and the foot soldiers was very important.

In 1950 the first electric watches were invented, which were quickly replaced by the quartz watches in 1970. These watches were almost in every way better than mechanicly driven watches. Yet interestingly the most expensive watches are still mechanicly driven watches.

The radio-controlled wristwatch correct itself every day. Governments could broadcast signals so that your clock was able to reset itself every day. These watches are pretty uncommen, since quartz watches work almost just as good as these one and they look better.

A few years ago atomic watches and smartwatches were invented. Atomic watches are so precise that they had an accuracy of one second in a 1000 years. Smartwatches are still new and improving. Most of them have a touch screen and they can some apps. The future primarily lies with these watches, but some people that still like mechanical watches are paying big money for them.
